Frequently Asked Questions

How do I make the battery last longer?

Consider turning the camera off when you aren't using it. You can also turn it off if it is on a tripod, as walking past the lends will trigger a motion sensor and prevent the camera from going into sleep mode. You can also turn on ECO Mode, which does not affect performance.
What is the camera's lifespan?

The Canon EOS RP has a rated shutter lifespan of 100,000 actuations. In other words, this camera will be able to take approximately one hundred thousand photos.
How many photos can this camera store?

This depends on the size of your memory card (SD card). This camera can hold memory cards up to 2 terabytes in size. A card of this size could store several tens of thousands of photos.
Where can I find more information about this camera?

Some visual tutorials focusing on the Canon EOS RP can be found on our Demo page. Technical details can be read on the Documentation page.
Does this product support image stabilization?

The stock Canon EOS RP does not support in-body image stabilization. However, this can still be achieved with optical lens-based stabilization using certain lens attachments.
Is this considered a good travel camera?

Yes, the Canon EOS RP is an excellent camera for travel. The stock camera is small and therefore portable, and its battery life can be extended with a battery grip and by adjusting power settings.
Images are blurry. How can I fix this?

Make sure that the lens is clean. When you take the picture, press the shutter button gently to minimize shaking. To stabilize the camera, consider using a tripod. A faster shutter speed can prevent blur in low-light environments.
